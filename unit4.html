Unit 4:

I believe that Thailand is a multi-national state. This is because according to Encyclopedia Britannica, about ⅘ of Thailand's population are ethnic Thai (“Southeast Asia - Urbanization, Settlement, and Ethnic Diversity”). In addition, Encyclopedia Britannica also states that in the “far southern provinces, … the people are mainly Malay-speaking Muslims” (“Thailand - Modernizing Reforms, Military Dominance, and Medical Tourism”).

After a military coup in May 2014, the current constitution at the time was suspended and the military leaders took power. And they appointed a 200 member council. The leader of said council as named interim prime minister (“Thailand - Constitutional Monarchy”).

Since the 1990s: New Aspiration Party, Democrat Party, National Development Party, Thai Nation, Social Action Party, and Thai Citizens' Party, People Power Party (“Thailand - Constitutional Monarchy”) Move Forward, Pheu Thai (Head).

Prayuth Chan-ocha has been Prime Minister of Thailand since 2014 — after the military stepped in to end high-tension conflict between political groups — Prayuth was the former army chief and played a large part in the 2006 military coup (Eldridge and Albert).

Current candidate of the Move Forward party is Pita Limjaroenrat (Head).

Countries that border Thailand are: Myanmar on the West and North-West, Laos on the North-East and East, Cambodia on the South-East, and Mainland Malaysia on the Southern tip. The rest of the borders are water borders, Andaman Sea on the West and the Gulf of Thailand on the East.

For a long time Thailand and Cambodia have fought for control over Preah Vihear Temple, which is a current World Heritage Site and is a historical religious site. The conflict is armed and the two armies exchange fire from time to time (International Committee of the Red Cross).
