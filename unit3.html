Unit 3:

97% of the population in Thailand speaks Thai (“Language data for Thailand”).

94% of the population is Theravada Buddhist, 4% is Muslim, and >1% is Christian or other religions (“What are the major religions in Thailand? | CountryReports”).

Thailand demographics: Thai 75%, Chinese 14%, other 11% (“Thailand Population and Demographics from Thailand”).

The three geographical regions where indigenous people in Thailand live are: the Chao Ley in small fishing communities in the South; as well as hunter-gatherers in the South, the Mani people; small groups within the Korat plateau in the Northeast & Eastern parts; many highland peoples more North and Northwest; and “Nine so-called ‘hill tribes’ are officially recognised: the Hmong, Karen, Lisu, Mien, Akha, Lahu, Lua, Thin and Khamu” (“Thailand”).

Holidays in Thailand include: New Year's Day, Makha Bucha (3/6/23), Chakri Day (4/6/23), Songkran (4/13/23 & 4/14/23), Coronation Day (5/4/23), Bridge Public Holiday (5/5/23), Visakha Bucha (6/3/23), Queen Suthida's Birthday (6/3/23), King Vajiralongkorn's Birthday (7/28/23), Buddhist Lent Day (8/1/23), The Queen's Birthday (8/12/23), Anniversary of the Death of King Bhumibol (10/13/23), Chulalongkorn Day (10/23/23), King Bhumibol's Birthday (12/5/23), Constitution Day (12/10/23), New Year's Eve (“Holidays and Observances in Thailand in 2023”).
